Enter the SnailThrone



Nice story and all, but what is this?

SnailThrone is a decentralized game where you raise your nest of snails with love and care, grow their numbers, then mercilessly sacrifice them to the great glory of the snail gods, which lets you become a god yourself, somehow.

Snails aren't known for their brainpower. Good thing you're here to guide them!

Give me the less flowery rundown.

Spend ETH to buy snails.

Sacrifice snails to claim the throne.

Stay on the throne long enough without anyone else kicking you off, and you win the pot.

Is that it?

Your snails continuously lay eggs, which you can hatch for even more snails. Who doesn't like more snails?

But the soulless monsters out there could instead feed their snail eggs to the FrogKing, for an ETH reward.

Simply holding snails entitles you to part of the ETH spent on any buy or hatch transaction.

You can also sell your snails back to the game contract.

Wait... Why would I buy snails only to sell them later?

The cost of snails rises and decreases, relative to their total supply. The more snails around, the higher the price.

With the right timing, you could buy snails low and sell them high.

You will also earn ETH between the time you buy and the time you sell if other players buy and hatch snails, as some of the ETH is distributed to snail holders.

SnailThrone is a smart contract on Ethereum. It will run exactly as designed, 24/7, without possible interference from cheaters or developers.

The code is open source and can be checked on Etherscan; which means even if *you* can't personally understand code, other people with the ability to do so could raise the alarm if there was anything wrong.

Will I become a millionaire from this game?

You could become a snail millionaire, if you hatch your eggs long enough.

Rewards are directly proportional to the volume of players involved. The more people involved, the more ETH up for grabs.

First, understand this is a zero-sum game and not an investment. The gains of any player come from the losses of another player.

You shouldn't play with ether you can't afford to lose!

What do I need to play?

As with most DApps, you will want to have the MetaMask extension or another compatible Ethereum wallet.

You will also need ETH, to pay for transactions as well as the game itself.

There is a "trial" version of the game available on the Ropsten network.

ETH is free on the Ropsten network, so you can familiarize yourself with the rules of the game in a safe environment.
